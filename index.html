Ethical Student is a food, fashion and social action lifestyle blog with an ethical focus.

There is a stereotype amongst older generations that students and young people in general don’t care about our communities, the environment, health issues, or politics. This isn’t true. Terms like ethical consumer, vegan and environmental activism are no longer automatically associated with the middle aged, middle-class socialist (...not that there is anything wrong with middle aged, middle-class socialists!).

More and more of us are no longer buying into the world of fast-fashion and fast-food.

Here's our predicament and where Ethical Student comes in...

Being a student, or young, or poor (or all three) and trying to live ethically can often seem at odds. It can feel like products that are organic/ vegetarian/ vegan/ fairtrade/ sweatshop-free/ environmentally-friendly don’t fit a student budget. As two uni students (with too much time on our hands) we want to be better consumers. But consciously making an effort to live more sustainably – without blowing our entire student loan – is hard.

We want to take the price-tag and difficulty out of ethical living.
